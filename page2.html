Risk Factors

You may be at higher risk for hypochondria if you:

  • Have allergies that cause a runny nose
  • Think your roommates don't wash their hands enough
  • Think your forehead "feels warm"
  • Coughed once, then panicked
  • Don't want to accept that you're tired because you're old
  • Are really looking for an excuse to cancel plans

Diagnosed By

Your doctor may be able to diagnose hypochondria by asking you if you think you have it. If you think you have it, you definitely do.
